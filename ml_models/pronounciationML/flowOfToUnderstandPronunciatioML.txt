🔊 STEP 2: Audio Processing
File: audio_loader.py

Using:
👉 librosa

What happens:

Converts audio → waveform (numbers)

Resamples to 16kHz

👉 Example:

"hello" → [0.01, -0.03, 0.2, ...]
🎼 STEP 3: Feature Extraction

These are core ML features 👇

🎵 (A) Pitch

File: pitch_extractor.py

👉 Measures:

How high or low voice is

Example:

Correct tone → good pronunciation

Flat tone → poor clarity

🎧 (B) MFCC (Most Important)

File: mfcc_extractor.py

👉 MFCC = Mel Frequency Cepstral Coefficients

Using:
👉 Mel Frequency Cepstral Coefficients

👉 It captures:

Sound shape (how words are spoken)

👉 This is used in:

Speech recognition

Voice assistants

🔋 (C) Energy

File: energy_extractor.py

👉 Measures:

Loudness of speech

Example:

Too low → unclear speech

Too high → distortion

🧠 STEP 4: Text → Phonemes
File: phoneme_extractor.py
phonemes = g2p(text)

Using:

g2p_en

NLTK

💡 What are phonemes?

👉 Smallest sound units:

"hello" → HH AH L OW
🔍 What NLTK does internally

NLTK helps with:

1️⃣ Tokenization
"hello how are you" → ["hello", "how", "are", "you"]
2️⃣ POS Tagging
hello → noun/verb
3️⃣ Used by g2p_en

👉 To convert words → phonemes correctly
